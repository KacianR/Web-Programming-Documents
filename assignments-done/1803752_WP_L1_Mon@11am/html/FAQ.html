FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is a cultural exchange Programme?

Exchange programmes are defined as arrangements where individuals from different countries visit each other's country. Programmes commonly have an education or cultural focus. The aims of these programmes can include strengthening links between individuals and improving foreign language skills.


How does the program work?

After students have been carefully screened for English language proficiency, grades, and overall suitability for the program, we provide placement for temporary work, placement in public or private high schools or university.. While on our program, exchange students live with a volunteer host family. Each student is assigned a personal ISE representative to provide supervision and assistance during their stay.

Is it only for university students?

Our varied program options are designed to give our student travelers the opportunity to develop deep, lifetime connections with previously unknown people and places. There are School Break programs, summer programs, programs by age.

Why should I go on this program?

For many students, the prospect of studying abroad is both exciting and terrifying. You will, however, be sure to experience a brand new culture, meet new people and grow as a person. Some reasons to do it are: a. Studying abroad will expand your employment opportunities b. Travelling to nearby countries is easy c. You'll make new friends from around the world d. You can take the opportunity to learn a new language e. You'll experience a completely new way of life
